PhD Research

From Policy Commitment to Institutional Enactment

Learner-centredness in Irish higher education

Shaun Ferns · Technological University Dublin

The problem

Learner-centredness is widely invoked in higher education policy and institutional discourse, but its practical meaning is often uneven, negotiated and context-dependent.

Core analytic focus

Institutional learner-centred commitments are interpreted, negotiated and operationalised within institutional settings through governance processes, professional roles, strategic priorities and lived academic practice.

Central research question

How is learner-centredness institutionally enacted in Irish higher education, and how do institutional processes align, translate or mediate national policy commitments?

Research design

1

National document and policy analysis

2

Comparative qualitative case study

3

Analytic documentary artefact

National document and policy analysis

This strand examines how learner-centredness is constructed within Irish higher education policy and sectoral discourse.

Comparative qualitative case study

The empirical strand examines how learner-centred commitments are interpreted, negotiated and operationalised within two institutional settings.

Practice-based element

The analytic documentary artefact is not a final dissemination product. It is integrated into the analytic process as a structured mode of representational inquiry.

Artefact as inquiry

Selection, omission, sequencing, juxtaposition, editing, voice and representation are treated as analytic decisions that can surface tensions and refine interpretation.

Expected contribution

  • Empirically grounded account of institutional enactment
  • Mechanism-oriented extension of policy enactment theory
  • Enactment mechanisms framework
  • Typology of enactment patterns
  • Institutional conditions map

Boundaries

The study does not seek to rank institutions, measure learner outcomes quantitatively, or represent the whole Irish higher education system through the two cases.

Current stage

The project is in early research development: refining the literature architecture, methodology, ethics planning and documentary research approach.
