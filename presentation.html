PhD Research

From Policy Commitment to Institutional Enactment

Learner-centredness in Irish higher education

Shaun Ferns · Technological University Dublin

The problem

Learner-centredness is widely invoked in higher education policy and institutional discourse, but its practical meaning is often uneven, negotiated and context-dependent.

Core analytic focus

Institutional learner-centred commitments are interpreted, negotiated and operationalised within institutional settings through governance processes, professional roles, strategic priorities and lived academic practice.

Aim

To examine how learner-centredness is institutionally enacted in Irish higher education by combining national document and policy analysis with a comparative qualitative case study of two institutions.

Central research question

How is learner-centredness institutionally enacted in Irish higher education, and how do institutional processes align, translate or mediate national policy commitments?

Research sub-questions

  1. How is learner-centredness constructed within national policy and sectoral discourse?
  2. How is learner-centredness interpreted and operationalised within two institutions?
  3. How do institutional actors describe governance, interpretive and relational processes?

Research sub-questions

  1. What recurring cross-case patterns and tensions can be identified?
  2. What enactment mechanisms and institutional conditions can be abstracted from the two cases?

Design logic

These questions require a design that can move between national policy discourse, institutional interpretation, participant accounts and practice-based representation.

Research design

1

National document and policy analysis

2

Comparative qualitative case study

3

Analytic documentary artefact

Methodology

Methodologically, this is a practice-based qualitative study combining national document and policy analysis, a two-institution comparative case study, and an analytic documentary artefact.

Why this methodology?

A standard qualitative case study would support institutional analysis, but would not fully account for the analytic role of the documentary artefact. Action research or design-based research would imply a stronger intervention or product-development focus than this project requires.

Other methodological options

  • Policy analysis only: strong for discourse, weak for institutional enactment.
  • Ethnography: rich but too large for a bounded two-case part-time PhD.
  • Realist evaluation: useful for mechanisms, but more evaluative than this inquiry needs.

Theoretical lens

The principal theoretical lens is policy enactment. The study does not treat policy as something simply implemented, but as interpreted, translated, negotiated and mediated within institutional settings.

Why policy enactment?

Policy enactment fits because the study is about translation rather than implementation. The question is not simply whether learner-centred policy is put into practice, but how learner-centredness becomes institutionally meaningful.

Other theoretical lenses

  • Implementation theory: potentially too linear for this study.
  • Institutional theory: useful support, but less directly focused on policy translation.
  • Sensemaking: valuable, but too actor-interpretation focused on its own.
  • Organisational change theory: useful, but may over-frame the study as change management.

Research position

The study is interpretive and relational. It treats the meaning of learner-centredness as produced through relationships between policy texts, institutional processes, participant accounts and practice-based representation.

My position in the research

I am positioned as an insider-practitioner researcher in Irish higher education. This brings contextual understanding and practice-based insight, but also requires reflexivity, ethical care and transparency about role, power and institutional sensitivity.

Why this position?

The study is not measuring whether institutions are learner-centred. It is examining how learner-centredness is understood, negotiated and enacted. That makes an interpretive-relational and reflexive insider-practitioner position more appropriate than a positivist, purely evaluative or intervention-led stance.

National document and policy analysis

This strand examines how learner-centredness is constructed within Irish higher education policy and sectoral discourse.

Comparative qualitative case study

The empirical strand examines how learner-centred commitments are interpreted, negotiated and operationalised within two institutional settings.

Practice-based element

The analytic documentary artefact is not a final dissemination product. It is integrated into the analytic process as a structured mode of representational inquiry.

Artefact as inquiry

Selection, omission, sequencing, juxtaposition, editing, voice and representation are treated as analytic decisions that can surface tensions and refine interpretation.

Expected contribution

  • Empirically grounded account of institutional enactment
  • Mechanism-oriented extension of policy enactment theory
  • Enactment mechanisms framework
  • Typology of enactment patterns
  • Institutional conditions map

Boundaries

The study does not seek to rank institutions, measure learner outcomes quantitatively, or represent the whole Irish higher education system through the two cases.

Current stage

The project is in early research development: refining the literature architecture, methodology, ethics planning and documentary research approach.

One-minute version

This is a practice-based qualitative study, theoretically framed by policy enactment, and positioned as interpretive-relational insider-practitioner research.
