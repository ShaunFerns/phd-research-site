Part 1

The research problem

The problem

Widely invoked, unevenly enacted

Learner-centredness is visible in policy and institutional discourse, but its practical meaning is often negotiated, contextual and uneven.

Core analytic focus

Institutional learner-centred commitments are interpreted, negotiated and operationalised within institutional settings through governance processes, professional roles, strategic priorities and lived academic practice.

Aim

To examine institutional enactment

The study combines national document and policy analysis with a comparative qualitative case study of two institutions.

Central research question

How is learner-centredness institutionally enacted?

How do institutional processes align, translate or mediate national policy commitments?

Research sub-questions

1. How is learner-centredness constructed within national policy and sectoral discourse?

2. How is learner-centredness interpreted and operationalised within two institutions?

3. How do institutional actors describe governance, interpretive and relational processes?

Research sub-questions

4. What recurring cross-case patterns and tensions can be identified?

5. What enactment mechanisms and institutional conditions can be abstracted from the two cases?

Methodology

Practice-based qualitative study

Combining policy and document analysis, a two-institution comparative case study, and an analytic documentary artefact.

Why this methodology?

Standard case study

Supports institutional analysis, but does not fully account for the artefact as analytic practice.

Chosen design

Connects policy discourse, institutional interpretation and documentary-making as representational inquiry.

Alternatives considered

Policy analysis only

Strong for discourse, weak for institutional enactment.

Ethnography

Rich, but too large for a bounded two-case part-time PhD.

Realist evaluation

Useful for mechanisms, but more evaluative than this inquiry requires.

Theoretical lens

Policy enactment

Policy is treated as interpreted, translated, negotiated and mediated within institutional settings.

Conceptual hinge

Not implementation.
Enactment.

Why policy enactment?

Implementation asks

Was the policy put into practice?

Enactment asks

How was policy interpreted, translated and made meaningful?

Other theoretical lenses

Institutional theory: useful support, but less directly focused on policy translation.

Sensemaking: valuable, but too actor-interpretation focused on its own.

Organisational change: useful, but may over-frame the study as change management.

Part 3

Research position and ethical design

Research position

Interpretive and relational

The study treats the meaning of learner-centredness as produced through relationships between policy texts, institutional processes, participant accounts and practice-based representation.

My position

Insider-practitioner researcher

This brings contextual understanding and practice-based insight, but requires reflexivity, ethical care and transparency about role, power and institutional sensitivity.

Ethical considerations

Ethics as part of the design

Participants

Consent · anonymity · confidentiality

Institutions

Sensitivity · governance · reputational risk

Researcher role

Power · boundaries · reflexivity

Ethics and the artefact

Layered consent

Participation in an interview is not the same as agreement for material to be used in multimodal or documentary form.

Boundaries

Not ranking

Not generalising
