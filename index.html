PhD Research Overview

From Policy Commitment to Institutional Enactment

Learner-centredness in Irish higher education through national document and policy analysis, comparative qualitative case study, and practice-based research.

Open presentation Read overview

Research focus

This PhD examines how learner-centredness is institutionally enacted in Irish higher education. The project is concerned with how learner-centred commitments are interpreted, negotiated and operationalised within institutional settings through governance processes, professional roles, strategic priorities and lived academic practice.

Aim

The overall aim of the study is to examine how learner-centredness is institutionally enacted in Irish higher education by combining national policy and sectoral documentary analysis, including systematic mapping of publicly available institutional documentation across Irish HEIs, with a comparative qualitative case study of selected institutional case sites.

Central research question

How is learner-centredness institutionally enacted in Irish higher education, and how do institutional processes align, translate or mediate national policy commitments?

Sub-questions

  1. How is learner-centredness constructed within national higher education policy and sectoral discourse in Ireland?
  2. How is learner-centredness interpreted and operationalised within selected institutional case sites?
  3. How do institutional actors describe the governance, interpretive and relational processes through which learner-centredness is enacted?
  4. What recurring cross-case patterns and tensions can be identified in the institutional enactment of learner-centredness, including tensions between institutionally defined and learner-responsive interpretations?
  5. What enactment mechanisms and institutional conditions can be abstracted from the selected case sites?

National policy and sectoral analysis

Examines how learner-centredness is constructed within Irish higher education policy and sectoral discourse, including public documentary mapping across Irish HEIs.

Comparative case study

Explores how learner-centred commitments are interpreted and enacted within selected institutional case sites.

Analytic documentary artefact

Develops a practice-based artefact as part of the analytic process, not simply as a dissemination output.

Methodology, lens and position

Methodology

Practice-based qualitative research design

Combines national policy and sectoral documentary analysis, comparative qualitative case study, and an analytic documentary artefact.

Theoretical lens

Policy enactment

Used because the study examines translation, interpretation, negotiation and mediation rather than linear implementation.

Research position

Interpretive-relational and insider-practitioner

Treats meaning as produced through policy texts, institutional processes, participant accounts and reflexive practice.

Why these choices?

The study needs a design that can move between national policy discourse, sector-wide public institutional documentation, selected institutional cases, participant accounts and practice-based representation. Policy enactment is used because the central concern is not whether policy is simply implemented, but how learner-centredness becomes institutionally meaningful in practice.

Developing the documentary practice

The precise form of the documentary artefact will be developed iteratively through engagement with policy, institutional and participant evidence. Selection, omission, sequencing, juxtaposition, image, sound, music, voice, silence and editing may operate as analytic and representational decisions, with their development documented as part of the research process.

Ethical considerations

Ethics are central to the design because the study involves institutional settings, participant accounts and a practice-based documentary artefact. Key considerations include informed and ongoing consent, confidentiality, appropriate anonymity or agreed attribution, data protection, role boundaries, power relations, and layered consent for any material considered for multimodal or documentary representation.

The public-facing site does not publish formal ethics materials, participant data, institutional records or supervisory documentation.

Use of this site

This site is a public-facing research explainer. It is intended as a reusable presentation space for conversations about the PhD. Formal proposal documents, registration records, supervision notes, ethics materials and research data are not published here.
